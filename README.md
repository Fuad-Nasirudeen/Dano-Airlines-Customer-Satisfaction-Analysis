# Dano-Airlines-Customer-Satisfaction-Analysis
Dano Airlines, a UK based airline, experienced a drop in customer satisfaction below 50% for the first time. This project analyzes passenger survey data to identify key drivers of dissatisfaction and provide data-driven recommendations to improve overall customer experience.

![Dano Airline](https://github.com/Fuad-Nasirudeen/Dano-Airlines-Customer-Satisfaction-Analysis/blob/main/Dana%20Image.jpeg)

## Objective
-	Identify factors influencing passenger satisfaction
-	Evaluate overall customer satisfaction
-	Compare satisfaction across travel types and customer segments
-	Recommend actionable strategies to improve satisfaction rate

## Dataset Overview
-	Source: Airline passenger satisfaction survey
-	Records: 120,000+ passengers
-	Scope: Passenger demographics, flight details, service ratings
- Key variables:
  
  -	Satisfaction
  -	Type of travel
  -	Customer class
  -	Service ratings (cleanliness, comfort, inflight service, delays)
  -	Arrival and Departure Delays

## Skills/ Concepts Demonstrated
- Data Cleaning and Transformation
- Data Visualization
- Quick measures
- Dashboard Automation

## Analysis Process
 
### Data Preparation
- Removed duplicates
-	Handled missing values
-	Standardized categorical fields
-	Converted satisfaction into binary classification (Satisfied / Not Satisfied)


### Analysis
-	Analyzed satisfaction rate by:
    - Travel type (Business vs Personal)
    -	Class (Business, Economy, Economy Plus)
    -	Service factors.
-	Compared average service ratings between satisfied and unsatisfied passengers
-	Evaluated average arrival and departure delays
-	Identified lowest-rated services contributing to dissatisfaction

## Business Impact
-	Identifies delay reduction as a key lever for improving satisfaction
-	Helps leadership prioritize customer experience investments
-	Supports data-driven decision-making to increase satisfaction rate

## Key Insights
-	Overall Satisfaction Rate is 43.45%
-	Business travelers (58.77%) are significantly more satisfied than personal travellers (10.13%)
-	Business class passengers (69.44%) report much higher satisfaction than Economy Plus and Economy
-	Average Delays exceed 14 minutes, negatively impacting satisfaction
-	Inflight service, seat comfort, and delays had the strongest impact on satisfaction
-	Personal travelers were less satisfied than business travelers
-	Poor service ratings strongly correlated with dissatisfaction

## Recommendation

- ### Reduce Flight Delays:
 
  Average arrival and departure delays exceed 14 minutes.

  Action: Improve schedule planning, turnaround efficiency, and real-time delay communication to passengers.
  
  Expected Impact: Higher punctuality will directly improve overall satisfaction.
- ### Improve Economy-Class Experience:
  
  Economy and Economy Plus satisfaction rates are below 25%, compared to 69.44% in Business Class.

  Action: Enhance seat comfort, cleanliness, and inflight service quality for Economy passengers.

  Expected Impact: Increases satisfaction among the largest passenger segment.
- ### Upgrade In-Flight WiFi:
  
  In-flight WiFi has the lowest service score (2.73).
  
  Action: Invest in faster, more reliable WiFi or set clearer expectations when WiFi is unavailable.
  
  Expected Impact: Improves perception of service quality, especially for business travelers.
- ### Target Personal Travelers with Value Improvements:

  Personal travelers show only 10.13% satisfaction.

  Action: Introduce value-based incentives such as bundled services, improved online booking experience, and better onboard comfort.

  Expected Impact: Raises satisfaction in the lowest-performing customer segment.
- ### Improve Arrival & Departure Convenience:

  Arrival and departure convenience scored (3.06 / 5), indicating moderate dissatisfaction.

  Action: Optimize check-in, boarding processes, and gate accessibility.

  Expected Impact: Enhances the overall travel experience and reduces frustration.

## Limitations
-	Survey data is subjective
-	No cost or operational data to measure feasibility of recommendations

## Conclusion
Customer satisfaction at Dano Airlines is heavily influenced by service quality and comfort. Improving inflight service, reducing delays, and enhancing economy-class experience can significantly improve satisfaction levels.
